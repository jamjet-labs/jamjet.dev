# Visual Brief — "What Your Competitors Are Already Doing With AI Agents"

*For designers: descriptions of each visualization referenced in the blog post.*

---

**1. competitors-timeline.svg** — Horizontal timeline from 2017 to 2026. Key milestones plotted: JPMorgan COiN (2017), BofA Erica (2018), Morgan Stanley AI Assistant (Sep 2023), Klarna AI launch (Jan 2024), Harvey $3B valuation (Feb 2025), Goldman Claude agents (Feb 2026). Show acceleration — milestones cluster more densely toward 2025-2026. Color-code by industry (finance = blue, legal = green, support = orange, insurance = purple).

**2. competitors-finance-table.svg** — Four-column comparison card: JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, Bank of America. Rows: "Key deployment," "Scale," "Key metric," "Started." Clean, scannable. Dark background, white text, monospace numbers.

**3. competitors-legal-adoption.svg** — Two-part visualization. Left: bar chart showing legal AI adoption jumping from 19% (2023) to 79% (2024). Right: side-by-side comparison showing firms with AI strategy are 2x more likely to see revenue growth, 4x more likely to see ROI. Use Thomson Reuters and Clio branding colors.

**4. competitors-klarna-journey.svg** — Three-panel horizontal layout. Panel 1 "Deploy" (green): 2.3M conversations, 2-min resolution, $40M projected savings. Panel 2 "Decline" (amber): quality drops, CSAT falls, CEO admits overcorrection. Panel 3 "Hybrid" (blue): AI handles 2/3 of chats, humans handle complex cases, rehiring begins. Arrow connecting all three. Honest, not sensationalized.

**5. competitors-support-comparison.svg** — Horizontal bar chart or comparison cards. Salesforce Agentforce: 83% resolution, 2M+ conversations, $50M savings. Intercom Fin: 41-67% resolution, 40M+ conversations. Zendesk: 80% claimed capability, 20K customers. Include reliability indicator (high/medium/low) based on source quality.

**6. competitors-insurance-pipeline.svg** — Before/after split. Left "Before": linear pipeline showing Application -> Risk Assessment -> Pricing -> Compliance -> Issuance, labeled "days to weeks." Right "After": compressed pipeline with AI at each stage, labeled "hours." Name companies at relevant stages: Lemonade (claims), Zurich (risk assessment), Allianz (underwriting guidance). Show the 14% -> 70% adoption projection.

**7. competitors-three-patterns.svg** — Three cards or icons. Card 1: "Start with one workflow" (single arrow, not a branching tree). Card 2: "Humans in the loop" (human + agent icon with feedback arrow). Card 3: "Production infrastructure" (checkpoint/durability icon). Clean, minimal, memorable.

**8. competitors-compounding-curve.svg** — Line chart. X-axis: quarters (Q1 2024 -> Q4 2026). Two lines: "Early mover" curves upward (compounding), "Late mover starting Q1 2026" starts flat and stays below. The gap between lines widens each quarter. Label the gap "organizational learning advantage." Not exponential hype — realistic, moderate compounding.

**9. competitors-getting-started.svg** — Three-step horizontal flow. Step 1: "Identify the painful handoff" (magnifying glass on a process diagram with a highlighted bottleneck). Step 2: "Require durable infrastructure" (checklist with 5 items: crash recovery, audit trails, human approval, model flexibility, protocol support). Step 3: "Design the human boundary" (human figure + agent figure with a clear dividing line labeled "judgment" on human side, "volume + complexity" on agent side). Clean, minimal, action-oriented. Should feel like a worksheet, not a diagram.
